Overview

Built to Resist Deliberate Forced Entry

SR3 marks the step from commercial security into high security. Doorsets at this level are independently tested by the Loss Prevention Certification Board against deliberate forced entry using heavier tools — crowbars, short axes and cordless drills (LPS 1175 tool category C) — sustained over a longer attack than SR2.

It's the rating specified where the target behind the door is genuinely valuable or critical: vault entrances, utility substations, data centres and secure stores. Single and double doorsets are outward opening, suit internal and external openings, and can be configured with louvre panels for ventilated plant applications — a combination substation and switchroom specifiers rely on.

Applications

Where SR3 Is Specified

  • Vault entrances — certified resistance where valuables are stored.
  • Utility substations — protecting electrical infrastructure, with louvre ventilation options.
  • Data centres — plant, comms and server room openings.
  • High-security areas — secure stores and restricted zones.
  • Healthcare facilities — pharmacies and controlled-drug stores.
  • Municipal buildings & warehouses — high-value and critical openings.
The Right Level?

When to Choose SR3

Choose SR3 when your risk assessment anticipates a prepared intruder who has planned the attack and brought serious tools — not just seized an opportunity. Insurers and security consultants commonly require SR3 for vaults, critical infrastructure and high-value stores.

If the threat profile includes sustained, experienced attack — or your specifier demands the highest rating in our range — see SR4. For general commercial openings, SR2 usually suffices and offers more configuration options.

FAQs

SR3 Questions

What does LPS 1175 SR3 protect against?

SR3 doorsets are independently tested to resist deliberate forced entry using heavier tools — crowbars, short axes and cordless drills (LPS 1175 tool category C) — over a longer sustained attack than SR2. It's high-security protection for genuinely valuable or critical targets.

Where is SR3 typically required?

Vault entrances, utility substations, data centres, high-security stores, healthcare drug rooms and similar critical openings — often driven by insurer requirements or a security consultant's specification.

Can SR3 doors be ventilated?

Yes — louvre panels are available at SR3, which is why the rating is widely used on substations, switchrooms and plant rooms that need certified security and airflow together. Overpanels are also available.

What sizes are available in SR3?

Single doorsets up to 1440mm × 3060mm and double doorsets up to 2510mm × 3060mm, outward opening, for internal or external fit.
