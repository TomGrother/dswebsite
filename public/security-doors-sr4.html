Overview

Maximum Certified Attack Resistance

SR4 sits at the top of our security range. Doorsets at this level are independently tested by the Loss Prevention Certification Board against sustained, experienced attack with serious cutting and levering tools (LPS 1175 tool category D) — the kind of assault mounted against critical national infrastructure and high-value targets.

SR4 is specified where failure is not an option: data centres, utility substations, secure healthcare stores and restricted high-security areas. Doorsets are outward opening in single and double configurations, with access-control hardware and louvre panels available — so even the most secure openings can integrate with building access systems and ventilation requirements.

Applications

Where SR4 Is Specified

  • Data centres — protecting servers, comms rooms and critical plant.
  • Utility substations — maximum-rated protection for energy infrastructure, with louvre ventilation options.
  • High-security areas — cash rooms, secure stores and restricted zones.
  • Healthcare facilities — the most sensitive controlled stores.

SR4 openings are usually part of a wider security strategy — our team regularly works alongside security consultants, insurers and infrastructure operators to meet a written specification exactly.

The Right Level?

When to Choose SR4

Specify SR4 when the threat model assumes a capable, equipped and determined adversary with time to work — or when a specification, insurer or infrastructure operator explicitly requires LPS 1175 SR4. For most commercial risk profiles, SR2 or SR3 provides certified protection at lower cost with more configuration options.

Note that maximum doorset dimensions are tighter at SR4 than lower ratings — check opening sizes early in design. Our team can advise on structural openings and site surveys.

FAQs

SR4 Questions

What does LPS 1175 SR4 protect against?

SR4 doorsets are independently tested against sustained, experienced attack using serious cutting and levering tools (LPS 1175 tool category D). It's the highest rating in our range, specified for critical infrastructure and high-value targets where the adversary is assumed to be capable, equipped and determined.

Where is SR4 required?

Typically where a written security specification, insurer or infrastructure operator demands it: data centres, utility substations, cash rooms, secure healthcare stores and restricted high-security areas.

Can SR4 doors integrate with access control?

Yes — access-control hardware is available at SR4, alongside personnel and panic hardware, so maximum-rated openings can still integrate with the building's access management system. Louvre panels are also available for ventilated plant applications.

What sizes are available in SR4?

Single doorsets up to 1284mm × 2752mm and double doorsets up to 2492mm × 2752mm — tighter maximums than lower ratings, so check structural openings early in design. All doorsets are outward opening for internal or external fit.
