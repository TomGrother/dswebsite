Overview

Enhanced Security, Maximum Flexibility

SR2 is the workhorse of the LPS 1175 scale — independently tested by the Loss Prevention Certification Board to resist a more determined attack with a wider tool set, including levers and cutting tools (LPS 1175 tool category B). For most commercial, education and healthcare applications, SR2 is the rating specifiers ask for.

It's also the most configurable doorset in our security range: vision panels, louvre panels, overpanels, access-control hardware and — unique at this level — an optional fire rating tested to EN 1634, letting one doorset satisfy both the security and fire strategy for an opening. Single and double doorsets reach 3100mm high, the largest in the range.

The Right Level?

When to Choose SR2

Choose SR2 where a risk assessment anticipates deliberate, prepared attack — someone arriving with tools — or where an insurer, police advisor or specification calls for certified security beyond entry level. It's the sweet spot of protection, configurability and price for most projects, which is why doorsets like our Watling Gardens SR2 project specify it for communal and residential security.

FAQs

SR2 Questions

What does LPS 1175 SR2 protect against?

SR2 doorsets are independently tested to resist determined forced entry using a wider tool set than SR1 — including levers and cutting tools (LPS 1175 tool category B). It's the most commonly specified certified rating for commercial, education, healthcare and residential-communal applications.

Can SR2 security doors also be fire rated?

Yes — SR2 is the rating in our range with an optional fire rating, tested to EN 1634. One doorset can then satisfy both the security requirement and the fire strategy for the same opening.

What configuration options does SR2 support?

The widest in our security range: vision panels, louvre panels, overpanels, and personnel, panic or access-control hardware — in single or double outward-opening doorsets up to 2800mm × 3100mm.

What's the difference between SR2 and SR3?

The tested threat level. SR2 resists a determined attack with category B tools; SR3 resists deliberate forced entry with heavier category C tools over a longer attack. If your site is higher-risk — vaults, substations, critical infrastructure — step up to SR3 or SR4.
